Neurons and Neural Networks

A neuron is a cell that takes in electrical input from one or more cells and outputs some electrical response.

A synapse is an electrical transfer between two neurons.

A neural network is a collection of neurons arranged in a network that takes in electrical input and outputs some electrical response and collectively performs some task, such as recognize a face, add two numbers together, or memorize a phone number.


This is the completely elaborated neural network of the C. Elegans worm. This organism, with only 302 neurons, is capable of associative and non-associative learning as well as complex series of movements, making it a model organism in neuroscience.

The basis of biological learning is neural plasticity.

Bioinspiration of Artificial Neural Networks

In 1958, Frank Rosenblatt created an algorithm called the perceptron, inspired by biological neural networks to acheive pattern recognition. Rosenblatt's algorithm could learn to add, and subtract. It wasn't until the discovery of the backpropagation algorithm by Paul Werbos in 1975 that the perception algorithm could acheive more complex patterns such as the exclusive-or logical operator.

However, artificial neural networks research slowed in the 1980's and 90's as computers were not powerful enough to process large neural networks and emerging simpler models briefly found greater accuracy. In the 2000's, neural networks rose to prominense again with the advent of deep learning.
